Bluedobie Developing Action Plan

Less noise. Better signal. A site and social system that makes Bluedobie easier to understand, trust, and hire.

This plan turns the June 2026 social analytics into a practical operating system for Bluedobie Developing: LinkedIn as the authority engine, Bluedobie Dialogues as the long-form thinking hub, the website as the conversion layer, and the Build Log as the receipts.

The plain read

Bluedobie does not need to be louder everywhere. It needs to be clearer in the places already showing signal. The numbers point to a LinkedIn-led strategy, a cleaner website path, and a sharper bridge between short-form posts, long-form articles, and service inquiries.

The core message: Bluedobie builds durable websites, practical AI systems, Shopify support, and small-business workflows for people who are tired of duct-taping their way through digital chaos.

Stop, start, continue

Stop

  • Treating every channel like it deserves equal time.
  • Using Gridiron Gazette as a business publishing destination. It is a fantasy football recap engine, not the Bluedobie content hub.
  • Over-investing in YouTube, Instagram, or Google Business Profile until they show business movement.
  • Posting interesting thoughts that do not connect back to trust, clarity, systems, or a service path.

Start

  • Making LinkedIn the primary short-form authority channel.
  • Expanding the strongest LinkedIn posts into Bluedobie Dialogues articles on Medium.
  • Building a dedicated Site & Systems Review offer as the low-friction entry point.
  • Turning portfolio work into Build Log entries that show the problem, system, and outcome.

Continue

  • Using Facebook as the warm community and trust layer.
  • Testing TikTok lightly for reach without building the whole plan around it.
  • Using X for low-effort distribution, networking, and link movement.
  • Letting Sabre and the human layer stay visible where it builds trust and memory.

The content architecture

The pieces need distinct jobs. When everything is called content, everything gets messy. Bluedobie needs a simple path from thought to trust to inquiry.

LinkedIn

Job: authority and conversation.

Short-form posts that name the problem clearly: websites that need explaining, Shopify operations that wobble after launch, AI tools that create more babysitting than relief.

Bluedobie Dialogues

Job: long-form thinking hub.

This is the Medium home for deeper product and systems articles. Two-thirds of the content should stay product, systems, workflow, web, Shopify, or AI focused.

Build Log

Job: receipts.

Project notes, behind-the-build decisions, before-and-after clarity, and proof that the thinking turns into usable systems.

Services

Job: hiring path.

Clear service pages that tell visitors what Bluedobie fixes, who it is for, what happens next, and how to start without a discovery-call obstacle course.

Primary content path: LinkedIn post → Bluedobie Dialogues article → service page or Site & Systems Review → Contact.

Core content pillars

Website clarity

Websites That Explain Themselves

Your website should not require a guided tour. This pillar covers homepage clarity, service-page structure, accessibility, portfolio proof, and why pretty is not the same thing as useful.

Shopify

Shopify Without the Panic Spiral

Shopify makes launch easy. Running the store is the real system. This pillar covers products, policies, apps, workflows, owner-friendly operations, and the stuff that breaks after the confetti.

AI + workflows

AI That Reduces Load

AI should not become another dashboard to babysit. This pillar covers practical automation, content systems, dashboards that explain instead of overwhelm, and human-centered support tools.

Proof

The Build Log

Show the work without turning every post into a sales pitch. What was broken? What was constrained? What changed? What got easier after the system was fixed?

Trust

The Human Layer

Sabre, disability-aware systems, solopreneur reality, and practical resilience all belong here. Not as decoration. As proof that Bluedobie builds for real people on real days.

Channel plan

LinkedIn: primary engine

Cadence: 3 posts per week.

Use LinkedIn for authority, service education, and decision-maker trust. The strongest June posts were practical and problem-aware. Keep that edge.

  • Monday: problem-aware authority post.
  • Wednesday: build log or lesson learned.
  • Friday: offer-adjacent post with a soft call to action.

Facebook: trust layer

Cadence: 2 posts per week.

Use Facebook for community, updates, local credibility, Sabre-adjacent trust, and reminders that there is an actual human behind the work.

Instagram: maintenance

Cadence: 1 post per week or 3-4 posts per month.

Use simple visual snippets, quote cards, before-and-after notes, and low-lift proof. Do not let Instagram become a second job.

TikTok: lightweight experiment

Cadence: 1 short video per week for 8 weeks.

Use screen recordings, fast website tips, Shopify cleanup notes, and practical AI examples. Keep production simple. The goal is signal, not performance art.

X: distribution and networking

Cadence: 2-3 short posts per week.

Repurpose LinkedIn observations, share Dialogues links, and reply where useful. Do not build the house on rented attention that is already declining.

YouTube: pause or reset

Cadence: pause for 90 days unless there is a specific library-style video to publish.

If revived, make it practical: website audit walkthroughs, Shopify operations checklists, or AI systems explainers.

Website redesign direction

The website should stop acting like a general brochure and become a decision-support system. Visitors should not have to work to understand what Bluedobie does, who it helps, or what to do next.

New website promise: Websites, Shopify support, and practical AI systems for businesses tired of duct tape.

Home

Clear positioning, strongest proof, service paths, and a primary call to action for the Site & Systems Review.

Services

Group by outcome: Websites That Explain Themselves, Shopify Without the Panic Spiral, AI + Workflow Systems, and Care & Maintenance.

Site & Systems Review

A focused entry offer for people who know something is off but do not yet know whether it is the website, store, content, or workflow.

Build Log / Case Studies

Replace vague portfolio browsing with proof: the problem, the constraint, the system, and the outcome.

Bluedobie Dialogues

The Medium hub for long-form web, business, product, AI, and systems thinking. This is where the deeper articles live.

Meet Sabre, About, Contact

Keep the human trust pages, but make the path cleaner. The contact page should be simple, low-friction, and specific enough to qualify the need.

90-day rollout

Days 1-30: clarify the offer and prepare the system

  • Rewrite homepage positioning around websites, Shopify support, practical AI, and small-business systems.
  • Define the Site & Systems Review offer.
  • Turn the strongest website-clarity post into a Bluedobie Dialogues article.
  • Build a 4-week LinkedIn calendar around website clarity, Shopify operations, and AI systems.
  • Add tracking for CTA clicks, contact page visits, and referral sources.

Days 31-60: redesign and publish

  • Redesign the homepage around the new service path.
  • Rebuild the services page by outcome instead of tool category.
  • Create the Site & Systems Review page.
  • Convert portfolio entries into Build Log-style proof.
  • Publish one Shopify-focused Bluedobie Dialogues article.

Days 61-90: optimize and tighten

  • Review LinkedIn reach, interactions, and site referral traffic.
  • Check whether contact and services page visits improved after the redesign.
  • Decide whether TikTok earned continued effort.
  • Publish one AI/workflow-focused Bluedobie Dialogues article.
  • Document one current project as a Build Log entry.

The working rule

Bluedobie does not need to become louder. It needs to become easier to understand, easier to trust, and easier to hire. The work is already pointing in that direction. Now the system needs to catch up.
